Problem: Department Duel

The Principal of VIIT wants to determine which department is better at programming: CSE or ACSE. The heads of both departments claim their students are the best. To resolve the debate, the Principal decides to conduct a coding contest.

The contest consists of 5 questions, each carrying 100 marks. The winner is determined by calculating the average percentage of scores for each department. The department with the higher average is declared the winner. If the number of scores provided is less than the total number of students in a department, assume the scores of the non-participants to be 0.

Input Specification:

  • input1: Two space-separated integers representing the total number of students in CSE and ACSE, respectively.
  • input2: An array of integers representing the scores of the participating CSE students.
  • input3: An array of integers representing the scores of the participating ACSE students.

Output Specification:

Return a string: "CSE", "ACSE", or "Tie" based on the contest results.

Example 1:

Input:

3 2
300 200 100
400 100

Output:

ACSE

Example 2:

Input:

4 4
200 300
150 250

Output:

CSE

Example 3:

Input:

3 3
300 300 300
300 300 300

Output:

Tie
